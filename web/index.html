for Dota 2

Advanced Hotkeys & Setup

Preface

A layout should make sense: mechanically and visually.

The more intuitive it is - the better for performance.

I'm fairly confident this is the best Dota 2 hotkey layout possible.

And I'm going to explain the logic behind it on this page.

p.s. it's quite extensive

Hotkeys Overview

  • Select Group 1: 1
  • Select Group 2: 2
  • Select Group 3: 3
  • Select All Other Units: ~
  • Select Courier: F1
  • Abilities: Q W E R T
  • Abilities (Self): Q W E R T + Alt
  • Abilities (Learn): Q W E R T + Ctrl
  • Items: A S D Z X C
  • Items (Self): A S D Z X C + Alt
  • Neutral: V
  • Neutral (Self): V + Alt
  • Quickbuy: B
  • Buy Sticky: N
  • Take from Stash: B + Alt
  • Teleport Scroll: G
  • Teleport Base: G + Alt
  • Open Shop: Alt + WheelUp
  • Courier Deliver: Alt + F1
  • Courier Burst: Alt + WheelDown
  • Attack: Spacebar
  • Stop or Hold: F

Alt modifier

Alt is our "self-cast" modifier.

Think of Alt as "to me".

This enables us to quickly and easily cast what we need on ourselves in battle.

Unit Select

We want to group all Unit Select keys close to each other.

In this layout we cluster them around 1

  • Select Group 1: 1
  • Select Group 2: 2
  • Select Group 3: 3
  • Select All Other Units: ~
  • Select Courier: F1

More on courier usage later.

Abilities

Abilities use the intuitive QWERT layout:

  • Abilities: Q W E R T
  • Abilities (Self): Q W E R T + Alt
  • Abilities (Learn): Q W E R T + Ctrl

Not everyone knows, but Ctrl + Ability # instantly levels up the ability.

This is very convenient, because you can do it instantly.

Abilities - Quick Cast

Quick Casts allow us to cast instantly with a key press, eliminating the need for a mouse click.

Everything should be on Quick Casts, with one exception - precise Area of Effect spells.

Notable examples: Black Hole, Nether Blast, Chronosphere, etc.

Because we need to aim these very carefully - it's better to keep them on Normal Cast.

Alternatively, check Triggered on key release in Quick Cast options.

This allows us to instantly fire the ability as it is on Quick Cast, but holding down the ability key will not fire it until we release it.

Abilities - Ability 6

Now, not every hero has Ability 6.

And when they do, sometimes it's important and sometimes not at all.

Y is not a good key for an important ability - it's too far.

So, because we do not have Select Group 4 bound, 4 is a surprisingly great key for one of the abilities.

  • Ability 4, 5 or 6: 4

For example, for Invoker I would use 4 for Invoke, which would mean my invoked abilities are R and T.

But for most heroes I use R for Ultimate Ability.

Items

Arguably, instant access to items is more important than instant access to abilities.

That's why we bind them all to Quick Cast.

And just like with abilities - we must have instant access to self-cast.

That's why we don't bind them to Alt to begin with.

We also want them to be intuitive and easily accessible.

That's why we bind our items right below the ability row.

  • Items: A S D Z X C
  • Items (Self): A S D Z X C + Alt

This way, all items are easily accessible, self-cast is easily accessible and the layout is visually coherent with the in-game interface.

You will never miss click anything this way.

Per-Item Overrides

Very few people even aware of this option, but it's the best.

This menu allows us to set Normal Cast per item, overriding Quick Cast slot binds.

Uncheck the following:

Observer and Sentry Wards

Gleipnir

Meteor Hammer

Fallen Sky

Regardless of their inventory slot these items will be on Normal Cast.

This is especially valuable for Observer and Sentry Wards, because we need to see the visual AOE indicator for placement and to double-tap to switch between Obs / Sentry.

Same logic for the other ones.

Neutral Item

The Neutral Item is the 7th item, so it follows the C to the right.

  • Neutral: V
  • Neutral (Self): V + Alt

Easily accessible and (almost) visually coherent.

Teleport Scroll

Teleport Scroll is an item.

Let's use a key that's close to items.

That's why we use G:

  • Teleport Scroll: G
  • Teleport to Base: G + Alt

Think of G as "Go Ground".

Alt + G follows naturally as the "Go to my base" key.

Despite the fact that on user interface Teleport Scroll is below the Neutral Item, this setup is very intuitive.

Do not bind Teleport Scroll to Quick Cast - use Normal Cast.

Quickbuy & Sticky

We buy items often.

And it follows that we should use the keys that are close to our item binds:

  • Quickbuy: B
  • Buy Sticky: N
  • Take from Stash: B + Alt

B for QuickBuy - makes sense to me.

Buy Sticky is next to Quickbuy, because we often use both at the same time.

Buy Now.

Shop & Courier

While we are on the topic of items, let's discuss buying them.

These keys are very unusual and should surprise most players.

  • Open Shop: Alt + WheelUp
  • Select Courier: F1
  • Courier Deliver: Alt + F1
  • Courier Burst: Alt + WheelDown

Alt + F1 makes sense for Courier Deliver, because "Courier to me".

Courier maintains its actions on Q W E R T.

Highly recommended to bind Courier Shield to R as its "Ultimate" ability.

R is also much easier to reach.

Attack, Stop

I've been using Spacebar for Attack and F for Stop with great success for more than 10 years in many games.

  • Attack: Spacebar
  • Stop or Hold: F

Attack on Spacebar is a fantastic bind.

Thumb is usually a very underutilized finger, so for such a frequent action - it's just great.

And having it right under abilities and items - just makes perfect sense.

Stop is displaced to the right, so that it fits between Items and Teleport Scroll.

If you think of S as Stop, think of F as Freeze, Forfeit or F*ck it.

Either way, I promise you will find these extremely comfortable.

Move, Directional Move

Directional Move is used a lot on heroes like Shadow Fiend and Slark.

It's a weird key, because it's not a priority, but 'spikes in value' - so needs easy access.

CapsLock is a great key for Directional Move, because it allows us to use all abilities and items while pressing it.

  • Directional Move: CapsLock

Move has a few use cases (for example to stick Juggernaut Bladestorm through the jukes), but overall it's the least used action button.

So that's why we are using Alt + F because that's what's left and it's somewhat related to movement.

  • Move: F + Alt

Talents

I found having a hotkey for Select Talent more comfortable than not having it.

Because Select Talent is related to Select, the next available key is F2.

  • Select Talent Menu: F2
  • Select Talent 1: 1
  • Select Talent 2: 2

Chat Wheel

This is not for flavor - this is a mandatory bind, and it has to be easily accessible.

Emotional Damage is a real thing, as is Emotional Support.

  • Chat Wheel: H

The buttons on the side of your mouse - if you have them - can be a good option as well.

Chat Wheel Setup

Over a decade I figured this is the required setup to play.

Towards the top we have progressively "aggressive" call-outs.

Towards the bottom we have progressively "defensive" call-outs.

Go and Get Back are obvious. Required for any coordination.

Fight Under Tower probably contributes to half of my MMR.

Synergizes perfectly well with every other call out.

It's the backbone of "DO NOT DO ANYTHING STUPID".

Prepares the team for action.

Reminds them not to dive.

Reminds them not to chase off your own high ground.

It's the best.

Spread Out is for any pre-teamfight action, especially when facing big ultimates such as Black Hole, Reverse Polarity, etc.

Generally useful around objectives, especially high-ground.

Used with Get Back or I'm retreating works as the "Please Disperse" call out to forfeit an objective so that everyone is free to push out lanes or get extra farm.

Group Up serves as the "Get Ready" and "Let's Go" call-outs.

It's also useful when you actually want to group up, i.e. for AOE heals or smokes.

On my way indicates our intent to join.

So it serves as "Wait for me" and "I'm not there yet" at the same time.

I'm retreating indicates our intent NOT to join.

These are great call-outs, because they lower the chance of our team overextending. Our team needs to know whether or not they can rely on us to make better decisions.

All miss is a great call-out for "They are smoked", "They are ganking", "Take a look at the minimap" and "Careful."

Use with Fight under tower, Get back and Group Up to bring your team into safety.
