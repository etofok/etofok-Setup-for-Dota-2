Preface

A layout should make sense: mechanically, visually and intuitively.

The more intuitive it is to operate - the better it is for our performance.

I'm fairly confident this is the best Dota 2 hotkey layout possible.

And I'm going to explain the logic behind it on this page.

p.s. it's quite extensive.

Alt

Think of Alt as our "self" modifier.

Alt = "to me".

We are going to use it very extensively.

Selectors

We want to group all Select Unit keys close to each other.

And we group all these around 1

  • Select Group 1: 1
  • Select Group 2: 2
  • Select Group 3: 3
  • Select All Other Units: ~
  • Select Courier: F1

Very simple so far.

More on courier usage later.

Abilities

Abilities use the intuitive QWERT layout:

  • Abilities: Q W E R T
  • Abilities (Self): Q W E R T + Alt
  • Abilities (Learn): Q W E R T + Ctrl

Not everyone knows, but Ctrl + Ability # instantly levels up the ability.

This is very convenient. Use it.

Now, not every hero has Ability 6

And when they do, sometimes it's important and sometimes not at all.

Y is not a good key for an important ability - too far away.

If you do not have Select Group 4 bound, 4 is a great key for one of the abilities.

  • Ability 5 | 6: 4

Items. Explanation

We must have instant (and easy!) access to self-cast.

That's why we CANNOT bind our items to Alt + Anything.

Alt + Q W E A S D is the "HoNTrash" solution for items, used by many.

There are 3 very big reasons why this solution is... bad.

  • 1. Slow, because we have to press 2 keys Alt + Ability
  • 2. We can't use Alt as a modifier.
  • 3. Consequently, we have to rely on double-tap to self-cast, which is very long and is prone to missclicks

There's a better way.

Items

Items are top priority in this game and we must have instant access to all of them.

Arguably, instant access to items is more important than that of abilities.

And just like with our abilities - we must have instant (and easy!) access to self cast.

That's why we bind our items right below the ability row.

  • Items: A S D Z X C
  • Items (Self): A S D Z X C + Alt

Alt is our "to me" modifier.

The Neutral Item is the 7th item, so it follows the C to the right.

  • Neutral: V
  • Neutral (Self): V + Alt

All items are easily accessible and the layout is visually coherent.

Quickbuy & Sticky

We quickbuy items often.

And it follows that we should use the keys that are close to our item binds:

  • Quickbuy: B
  • Buy Sticky: N
  • Take from Stash: B + Alt

B for QuickBuy.

Our Buy Sticky is next to Quickbuy, because we often use both at the same time.

So this is very convenient.

Teleport Scroll

Teleport Scroll is an item.

Notably, it is visually above the Neutral Item.

So, it follows that we should use a key that's close to items and above the Neutral Item.

That's why we use G:

  • Teleport Scroll: G
  • Teleport to Base: G + Alt

G stands for "Go Get Ground", and I find this very intuitive and easy to remember.

Alt + G follows naturally as the "Go to MY base" key.

Shop & Deliver

While we are on the topic of items, let's discuss buying them.

  • Open Shop: Alt + WheelUp
  • Courier Deliver: Alt + WheelDown
  • Courier Burst: Alt + F1

These keys are very unusual and should surprise most players.

But I found this synergy to be the best, by far.

This works because we do our "buying routines" in a quick "swing" by holding Alt.

Buying Routine 1

  • 1. Alt + WheelUp to Open Shop
  • 2. Buy things. You can buy things while holding Alt
  • 3. Alt + WheelDown to Courier Deliver
  • 4. Alt + F1 to activate Courier Boost
  • 5. Done.

    • Buying Routine 2

  • 1. B to Quickbuy
  • 2. N to Buy Sticky for Teleport Scroll
  • 3. Alt + WheelDown to Courier Deliver
  • 4. Alt + F1 to activate Courier Boost
  • 5. Done.

    • Courier

    Now, the logistics for the items.

    • Select Courier: F1
    • Courier Burst: F1 + Alt
    • Courier Shield: F1 → R

    F1 for Select Courier is great, because now we have all our Select Unit actions very close to each other.

    Courier maintains its actions on Q W E R T,

    but it's better to bind Courier Shield to R as its "Ultimate" ability.

    We are handling Courier Burst with Alt + F1.

    Command Card

    I have been using Spacebar for Attack and F for Stop with great success for more than 10 years in many games.

    • Attack: Spacebar
    • Stop or Hold: F
    • Move: F + Alt
    • Directional Move: CapsLock

    Directional Move is used a lot on heroes like Shadow Fiend and Slark.

    It's weird key, because it's not a priority, but 'spikes in value' - so needs easy access.

    CapsLock is a great key for Directional Move, because it allows us to easily use all abilities and items while pressing it.

    Move has a few use cases (i.e. to stick Juggernaut Bladestorm).

    So we are using Alt + F because why not..
